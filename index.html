Gestational Diabetes (also known as Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)

What is Gestational Diabetes?

Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) is a condition in which a hormone made by the placenta prevents the body from using insulin effectively. Glucose builds up in the blood instead of being absorbed by the cells (www.hopkinsmedicine.org)

As a pregnant woman, it is pertinent to have certain health information. Undoubtedly, this information is vital for the overall health of both the mother and the baby. A pregnant woman needs to know about a condition known as Gestational Diabetes.

What does this disorder mean?

It is a condition in which a pregnant woman without diabetes develops high blood sugar levels during the period of the pregnancy. Gestational diabetes mellitus according to Keenan-Lindsay (2020), GDM is not a permanent disorder as glucose level mostly returns to normal around six weeks after delivery (p. 109). Generally, this condition results in a few symptoms (departure from normal feelings) but has many complications

Complications:

These include: Preeclampsia- this is suspected when high blood pressure and signs of damage to another organ system, most often the liver and kidneys are observed. Usually, it begins after 20 weeks of pregnancy in women whose blood pressure had been normal.

Stillbirth- this means death or loss of a baby before or during delivery. Even though there are many other medical reasons for this, but it is one of the complications of DGM.

Depression- (the most common form is known as Perinatal depression)- is depression that happens during pregnancy or in the first year after having a baby. It is a medical condition that causes feelings of sadness and a loss of interest in things one likes to do. It can affect how one feels, think and act and can interfere with one's daily life. It needs treatment to get better.

Increased risk of requiring a cesarean section (or C-section)- which means the use of surgery to deliver the baby). The size of the baby is the primary factor the healthcare provider uses to determine whether an option of having a vaginal delivery. Gestational diabetes can make the foetus grow more massive than usual, thus increasing the risk of requiring a cesarean section.

Causes of GDM:

What causes gestational diabetes? Gestational diabetes occurs when your body can't make the extra insulin needed during pregnancy. Insulin, a hormone made in your pancreas, helps your body use glucose for energy and helps control your blood glucose levels.

Gestational diabetes diet

• Plenty of whole fruits and vegetables.

• Moderate amounts of lean proteins and healthy fats.

• Moderate amounts of whole grains, such as bread, cereal, pasta, and rice, plus starchy vegetables, such as corn and peas.

• Fewer foods that have a lot of sugar, such as soft drinks, fruit juices, and pastries.
